The Client - Who They Are and Where They Started

Our client (name withheld at their request) is a mid-sized interior design and home renovation firm based in Jaipur, operating for over six years. They had a solid reputation from referrals, a team of 12, and a decent portfolio of completed projects. On paper, they were a healthy business.

But their digital presence told a completely different story. They had been running Google Ads for almost eight months with a monthly spend of ₹25,000 - and generating fewer than 15 leads per month. Their website had been built three years ago and hadn't been touched since. Their Instagram had 2,400 followers and an engagement rate under 0.8%.

The owner came to AdMediaX with a single, honest question: "We're spending money every month and it's just disappearing. What are we doing wrong?"

When we ran our initial audit, the problem wasn't their budget and it wasn't their product. It was that every single element of their digital funnel - the ads, the landing page, the follow-up process - was working against them. Individually, each issue seemed small. Together, they were quietly destroying every lead before it had a chance to convert.

What Our Audit Found - The Five Problems

Diagnosed Issues - Before AdMediaX
  • Google Ads were targeting broad, high-competition keywords like "interior design" instead of high-intent local terms like "interior designer Jaipur" or "home renovation cost Jaipur" - spending budget on clicks from outside Rajasthan
  • The landing page loaded in 6.8 seconds on mobile and had no headline - visitors landed on the homepage with no clear message, no testimonials, and no visible CTA above the fold
  • The contact form had 9 fields including "How did you hear about us?" - most mobile users abandoned it before submitting
  • Zero follow-up automation - leads that did come in were manually followed up "when someone had time," often 24–72 hours later
  • No retargeting - visitors who browsed the portfolio and left were never shown another ad, meaning the business was paying for awareness it never converted

None of these were unusual problems. We see variations of this exact combination in the majority of SMBs we audit across Jaipur and Rajasthan. The good news: every single one of them was fixable - without increasing the monthly ad budget by a single rupee.

The Strategy We Built - Week by Week

W1
Week 1

Foundation

Landing page overhaul + ad account restructure

Built a dedicated landing page from scratch - loaded in 1.9 seconds, led with a specific headline ("Jaipur's trusted home renovation experts - free consultation this week"), added 3 client testimonials above the fold, and reduced the contact form to 3 fields (name, phone, project type). Simultaneously restructured the Google Ads account into tightly themed ad groups targeting local, high-intent keywords only.

IT + Web Paid Ads
W2
Week 2

Automation

Lead follow-up sequence + WhatsApp integration

Set up an automated 3-step follow-up sequence: an instant WhatsApp message with a portfolio link (triggered within 90 seconds of form submission), a follow-up SMS at the 2-hour mark if no reply, and an email the following morning with a before/after project showcase. Average response time dropped from 38 hours to under 4 minutes.

Automation CRM
W3
Week 3

Retargeting

Meta retargeting campaigns + lookalike audiences

Launched Meta retargeting ads targeting everyone who had visited the website in the last 30 days but hadn't submitted a form. Also built a lookalike audience from the client's existing customer list (uploaded as hashed data) and ran a separate awareness campaign to this high-potential cold audience. Creative featured actual completed projects with specific location tags ("Recent project - Vaishali Nagar, Jaipur").

Meta Ads Retargeting
W4+
Weeks 4–8

Optimisation

Weekly data reviews + budget reallocation

Every week, we reviewed which ad groups, audiences, and creatives were generating the lowest cost-per-lead. Budget was shifted away from underperformers within 48 hours. By week 6, three high-performing ad variations were responsible for 70% of all leads - we scaled budget into those and paused the rest. This compounding optimisation is what most businesses never do - they set campaigns and forget them.

What Any Similar Business Can Take Away From This

1
More budget is almost never the answer before the funnel is fixed. This client's problem was never the amount they were spending - it was that every rupee was leaking through gaps in the landing page, the form, and the follow-up process. Audit before you scale.
2
Follow-up speed is a massive, underrated lever. Responding to a lead within 5 minutes vs 24 hours is not a small difference - it is the difference between a warm conversation and a cold one. Automate this. It costs very little to set up and the impact is immediate.
3
Local specificity in ad creative dramatically outperforms generic creative. Ads that mentioned Jaipur neighbourhoods by name - Vaishali Nagar, Malviya Nagar, C-Scheme - outperformed generic "interior design" ads by 3x on click-through rate. Your audience wants to feel seen, not targeted.
4
Retargeting is the most underused tool in the Indian SMB marketing stack. Most businesses pay to bring visitors to their website and then never show them another ad. This is like a shopkeeper letting a customer browse, leave, and never trying to bring them back. Set up retargeting - even at ₹3,000–5,000/month - before anything else.
5
Weekly optimisation compounds faster than most people expect. The gap between month-one results and month-two results in this campaign was 40% - not because the strategy changed, but because bad-performing ad variations were paused and budget was concentrated into what was proven to work. Data-driven decisions made weekly beat set-and-forget campaigns every single time.
